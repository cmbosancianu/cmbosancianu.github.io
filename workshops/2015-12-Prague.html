Multilevel Modeling with Stata

This intensive 3-day workshop was done at the Institute of Sociology, Czech Academy of Sciences, Prague, Czech Republic. 12 sessions were split up into 6 theoretical ones, and 6 laboratory-based ones. We managed to cover a wide range of multilevel specifications, from standard 2-level linear models, to a few generalized linear specifications (dichotomous, ordinal, categorical and count outcome variables).

All sessions were run with Stata. Due to the proprietary nature of the data used in the sessions, data files and Stata syntax files cannot be shared here.

Day 1

We started from the case of linear regression, and how it breaks down in the presence of clustered data. In the second session we cover basic notation for a 2-level MLM, along with ICC and estimation.

Day 2

We cover more advanced topics in the first session, including model fit statistics used in multilevel modeling, model comparisons, presenting results in an intuitive way, and centering data. The second session is devoted to a quick foray into 3-level specifications, followed by generalized linear models, and the specific case of dichotomous dependent variables.

The final two sessions are devoted to extending the generalized linear mixed-effects framework into the domain of ordered and categorical dependent variables, as well as count outcomes.
