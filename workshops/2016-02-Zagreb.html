Introductory Time Series Analysis with R/Stata

Materials for a three-day intensive workshop on introductory time-series analysis. The sessions were organized by Prof. Daniela Širinić and held at the Faculty of Political Science, Zagreb University. A copy of the schedule and reading list can be found here [PDF]. Zoltán Fazekas and Federico Vegetti were co-instructors. They handled the first two parts of the workshop and were tremendously helpful with software troubleshooting during the sessions I taught.

Slides, code files in Stata and R, as well as data sets are listed under each session. R code was produced by myself, while Federico generated most of the Stata code. I am indebted to him for the considerable help he volunteered.

The main data for the time-series component of the workshop comes from the Comparative Agendas Project, focusing only on the series for the United Kingdom. Smaller data sets used throughout comes from the MARPOR project, or was simulated by myself.

Session 1

We covered basic terms from time-series analysis: stationarity, auto-correlation, moving average and auto-regressive processes. We also looked at the ACF and PACF, as tools of diagnosing the type of processes we are observing.

Session 3

We learn how to estimate very simple AR and MA processes, and finish up with a composite ARMA process. The data is the UK series used in the first session, as well as a smaller series with exchange rates between New Zealand and UK. In this session we cover auto-regressive distributed lag (ADL) models and special cases of these very general specifications. We finish off with vector auto-regressive specifications (VAR). The data used is still the UK series from the CAP.
