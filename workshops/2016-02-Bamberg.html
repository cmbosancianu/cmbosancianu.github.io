Materials for a one-week long course in Introduction to Multilevel Modeling, at the 2016 ECPR Winter School in Quantitative Methods, University of Bamberg, Germany (instructor: Levi Littvay). Code files were prepared in R, Stata and SPSS.

Day 3

The data used today comes from the 6th wave of the European Social Survey, focusing on the determinants of turnout in a cross-national setting. The primary variable of interest at the country-level is income inequality, measured with the Gini index of inequality.

Day 4

The data used today comes from Ronald H. Heck, Scott L. Thomas, and Lynn N. Tabata's 2010 Routledge monograph (Multilevel and Longitudinal Modeling with IBM SPSS). The data measures 8670 students, randomly sampled from 491 schools, at 3 different points in time, with a standardized test. The focus is on predicting the individual-level trajectory in test scores over time.

Day 5

The data used today comes from Joop J. Hox's 2010 Routledge monograph (Multilevel Analysis: Techniques and Applications). The data is made up of 1000 students, who attended 100 different primary schools, and then 30 secondary schools. The focus is on academic achievement, as measured with a test score. We are trying to use predictors both at the secondary and primary school level.
