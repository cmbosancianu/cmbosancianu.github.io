About myself

I'm a PhD candidate in Comparative Politics at the Central European University, Budapest. In my work I focus on the effects of income inequality at the aggregate level on individual-level political attitudes and behaviors, and how this further impacts party dynamics in advanced industrial democracies. I frequently rely on R, LaTeX and Emacs for my work, and on coffee to get me through the headaches caused by the first three.

If you're interested in my work, please check the following links for an updated CV, copies of papers I'm currently working on or which have been already published, materials from some of the workshops I've held or assisted with over the years, presentation slides, R scripts which I find useful in my work, and a collection of links (mostly about statistics) with helpful information.

Thanks for stopping by!
