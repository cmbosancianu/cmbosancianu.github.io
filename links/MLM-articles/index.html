  1. A collection on papers for multilevel modeling is maintained by the IDRE at UCLA.
  2. The Centre for Multilevel Modeling at Bristol University. A great site for finding tutorials, papers, or software.
  3. A gentle introduction into the topic of cross-classified and multiple membership models is to be found in Antony Fielding and Harvey Goldstein, 2006, "Cross-classified and Multiple Membership Structures in Multilevel Models: An Introduction and Review", Research report no. 791, Department of Education and Skills, Institute of Education, University of London.
  4. A very good article on centering predictors in multilevel models: Craig K. Enders and Davood Tofighi, 2007, "Centering predictor variables in cross-sectional multilevel models: a new look at an old issue", Psychological Methods 12(2): 121-138.

  6. It can be the case in analyzing TSCS data that a relationship between DV and IV is valid only cross-sectionally and not longitudinally as well. This point is why I think Malcolm Fairbrother, 2013, "Two Multilevel Modeling Techniques for Analyzing Comparative Longitudinal Survey Datasets", Political Science Research and Methods, 2(1): 119-140, is a very good piece (preprint).

There are probably more topics which can be covered here, such as the use of weights in multilevel models (e.g. Tihomir Asparouhov, 2006, "General Multi-Level Modeling with Sampling Weights", Communications in Statistics - Theory and Methods, 35(3): 439-460, (preprint). Unfortunately, they tend to be heavily technical pieces, sometimes quite confusing for someone without a mathematical background such as myself.
